Dissertation

My dissertation examines drought adaptation, local strategies, and public health in the Southern African Development Community (SADC), with a focus on Namibia.

Chapter 1: Navigating Dry Spells

Scoping Review · SADC Region

This chapter synthesizes evidence on household and community drought-adaptation strategies across the SADC region, mapping how livelihoods, social systems, and institutions respond to recurrent climate stress.

Chapter 2: Framing Drought

Media Analysis · Namibia

This chapter analyzes media representations of drought adaptation in Namibia, exploring how narratives shape public understanding, political framing, and policy responses to slow-onset disasters.

Chapter 3: Local Strategies, Perceptions, and Impacts

Qualitative Fieldwork · Namibia

Through interviews and community-level insights, this chapter examines how households perceive drought, assess government responses, and adapt to changing environmental conditions.
